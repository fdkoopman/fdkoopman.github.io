Abstract

Diffusion policies produce high-quality, multi-modal action trajectories, but their iterative sampling makes them impractical for real-time robot control. We introduce Factorized Koopman Distillation (FKD), a student architecture that distills a multi-step diffusion teacher into a one-step controller by lifting states into a learned Koopman embedding and factorizing rollout dynamics into a dynamic latent transition and a lightweight action head. A small number of refinement steps \(J\) can optionally be applied at inference.

On D4RL MuJoCo locomotion, FKD matches or exceeds the diffusion teacher's normalized return while reducing decision-time latency from ~100–300 ms to ~1 ms, and it preserves the teacher's multi-modal behavior — a property prior one-step Koopman distillation (KDM / KDM-F) fails to reproduce, as we show both quantitatively and qualitatively.

Walker2d tolerates larger J, with a small but consistent improvement in return and worst-case return at J=4, at the cost of roughly 2× latency. HalfCheetah is non-monotonic: J=1 already matches the best re-trained setting, and J=3 is notably degraded. Extra refinement is therefore task-dependent; one-step deployment remains the strongest accuracy–latency trade-off on HalfCheetah.
